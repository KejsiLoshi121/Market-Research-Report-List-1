<p><p>The polycarbonate membrane market is segmented into hydrophilic and hydrophobic types, each serving distinct applications. Hydrophilic polycarbonate membranes are designed to attract water, improving filtration processes in water treatment and biotechnology by allowing selective permeability. Conversely, hydrophobic polycarbonate membranes repel water, making them ideal for gas filtration and certain medical applications, where moisture exclusion is essential. Both types leverage polycarbonate's durability and chemical resistance, catering to industries such as pharmaceuticals, electronics, and environmental management.</p></p>
