<p><p>The automotive high-performance brake system market primarily consists of disc brakes and drum brakes. Disc brakes, known for their superior heat dissipation and stopping power, are widely favored in high-performance vehicles due to their responsiveness and reduced fade during intense braking. In contrast, drum brakes, characterized by their enclosed design, offer effective braking in less demanding applications and are typically more cost-effective. Both types serve distinct roles, with disk brakes dominating in performance-centric markets, while drum brakes maintain relevance in budget-friendly segments.</p></p>
