<p><p>Otorhinolaryngology electrosurgical units (ESUs) are specialized devices used in ENT surgeries to cut tissue and control bleeding. The market is categorized into three main types: monopole electrosurgical units, which use a single active electrode for cutting and coagulation; bipolar electrosurgical units, which utilize two electrodes for precise tissue handling and reduced thermal spread; and vessel sealing units, designed for securely sealing blood vessels to minimize bleeding during and after procedures. Each type serves distinct surgical needs.</p></p>
